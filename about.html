About

The person behind the kit. And her team.

I'm Anat Weissman. My whole career is one skill, applied everywhere: I walk into a room, listen for twenty minutes, and see where the dots don't connect. Product says one thing, sales promises another, marketing sells a third, and the customer hears the noise. I've done this across industries and cultures, from Nairobi to boardrooms in Tel Aviv and beyond, building the decks, running the teams, surviving contact with reality.

The pattern is always the same. Companies don't lose deals because the product is weak. They lose them because the story fractures, and nowhere does it fracture more quietly than at the Sales to CS handoff, where the promise that won the deal gets retold by someone who never heard it.

StoryOS is not a product I imagined. It's the system I actually work with, packaged.

I run my own business with a team of AI agents. Not a metaphor and not a demo: my operations, my content, my research run through named agents with defined jobs, a shared memory, and one hard law between them and the world: they draft, I judge. Nothing ships that can't back its claims. I built that law into my own team first, because my entire brand stands on saying only what I can prove. Then I built the same architecture for the one function that lives and dies on the story holding: enablement.

So when the kit flags a claim as [VERIFY] instead of inventing a number, that's not a feature I added for the sales page. It's the rule I work under every day, shipped to you.
